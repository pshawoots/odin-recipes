Rice Wine aka Chaang

Chaang is an alcoholic drink traditionally created using Barley but overseas Tibetans nowadays use Rice.

Here is link to a video that you can follow along to if you want.

Ingredients

  • 5 cups Jasmine Rice
  • 5 cups of Water
  • 1 Table spoon of dry yeast

Prep

  1. Wash your hands.
  2. Wash and dry a container large enough to put the rice and water in for fermentation.
  3. Prep a clean surface to lay down your cooked rice.

Instructions

  1. Cook your rice as you normally would. We use a rice cooker, with 5 cups of jasmine rice, and water filled to the 5 cup line.
  2. Once the rice is cooked, stir around the rice in the rice cooker or pot your cooked it in. You want to sort of loosen and fluff the rice up – you don’t want it packy or clumpy at all.
  3. Spread the rice onto your very clean working surface to cool it. Work through the rice when it is cool enough to touch, loosening up any clumpy bits.
  4. Allow the rice to cool until its warm to the touch.
    • If the rice is too hot when you add the yeast, you will get sour chang.
    • If the rice is too cold, it will take longer to ferment, which is okay, if you have time.
  5. Sprinkle the dried yeast over the rice and mix it in very well.
  6. Pour the rice mixture in your prepared container.
  7. Cover it with a lid.
  8. Swaddle the container like a baby in a couple of warm blankets.
  9. Leave it for 4-5 days and do not open it in the mean time.
  10. After 7 days open it and you'll notice a fermented and a bit alcoholic smell.
  11. Add 3 cups of water to the container and leave in a refrigerator.
  12. After 24 hours it is ready! You can keep it in there for longer if you want.
